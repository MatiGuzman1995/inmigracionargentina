Frequently Asked Questions

What type of residency can I apply for in Argentina?

There are temporary, permanent, and family-based residencies. Our team will advise you based on your nationality and personal situation.

Can I work while my residency is being processed?

Yes, as long as you have a residency-in-process certificate issued by Immigration.

What is the difference between residency and Argentine citizenship?

Residency allows you to live and work legally in Argentina; citizenship grants you the same rights as an Argentine citizen (passport, voting rights, etc.).

Do my documents need to be translated into Spanish?

Yes, all foreign documents must be translated by a certified public translator. Our team provides this service.

What is a Hague Apostille and what is it used for?

It’s an international certification that validates your documents in Argentina. It must be issued in the same country where the document was created. We can guide you through this process if needed.

Are you a government agency?

No, we are a private consultancy specialized in immigration procedures and official translations. We do not represent the National Immigration Office.

Do you work with foreigners who are not yet in Argentina?

Yes, we assist both individuals who are already in the country and those who are planning to travel soon.

Do you offer assistance in other languages?

Yes, we provide support in Spanish, English, and French.

How much does the service cost?

The price depends on the type of procedure and the complexity of each case. We offer personalized quotes with no obligation.

Can I complete all procedures from outside the country?

Some procedures require physical presence, while others can be started from abroad. We recommend contacting us so we can guide you according to your specific case.
